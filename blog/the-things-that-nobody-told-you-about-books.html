The things that nobody told you about books

It may be one of two cases, either you're an avid reader I'm talking to, or someone who has hated books all their life. I started reading quite late, up until I was 13 the only things that I had read were school textbooks. I had quite a bit of books in my home, but I had no interest in reading those. They were my father's. Encyclopedias always got my attention, they had pictures afterall. I would wonder what these people might be doing staring at those plain pages for hours on end, wasn't it boring.

There was definitely something these people knew, something that they had experienced which I hadn't. I knew that they knew more than me, there started the itch. I went to my school library, borrowed a book, a story book. I started reading it, read a few pages everyday. But I got bored, couldn't finish it. I just left it and returned it. I couldn't get why I wasn't able to read a book!

While this time in my life I had made a friend who used to read(A LOT!). I was fascinated by how he could finish 500-800 paged books in a single day. This person was on a whole new level, he didn't borrow a single book at a time, he borrowed a dozen! Sometimes I had to carry some of them. Our librarian was lovely to believe him and allow him to issue books on other student's library cards. It is very honestly said that you look for yourself in the world. We admire people on the basis of who we are. Perhaps I was seeing myself in him, I wanted to be like him. Though I never issued a dozen books from the library.
